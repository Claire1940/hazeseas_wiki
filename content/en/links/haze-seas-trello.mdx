<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Devil Fruits: Spawn Timers & Rarity Rankings">

Devil Fruits are the cornerstone of any Haze Seas build, offering unique abilities categorized into Paramecia, Logia, and Zoan types. Understanding the spawn mechanics is vital for players who prefer farming fruits over purchasing them with Robux. In a standard server, a fruit spawns every **60 minutes** at random locations across the islands. If not found, the fruit will despawn after **20 minutes**.

The game features a roster of 34 fruits, ranging from common utility types like Spin and Chop to mythical powerhouses like Dough and Dragon. Logia fruits are particularly prized by beginners because they provide elemental intangibility, making the user immune to physical attacks from NPCs that are at a lower level or do not possess Haki.

| Fruit Name | Rarity | Type | Primary Use Case |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Dough** | Mythical | Paramecia | High-tier PvP and boss combos |
| **Dragon** | Mythical | Zoan | Transformation and massive AoE |
| **Phoenix** | Legendary | Zoan | Healing, sustain, and flight |
| **Magma** | Rare | Logia | Best fruit for boss damage/farming |
| **Light** | Rare | Logia | Fastest travel and ranged grinding |
| **Ice** | Rare | Logia | Crowd control and sea walking |
| **Bomb** | Common | Paramecia | Early game explosive damage |

<Callout type="warning" title="Important">
In VIP servers, there is a **5-fruit spawn limit**. Once five fruits have spawned and remain uncollected, no new fruits will appear until the existing ones are picked up or despawned.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Swords} title="Combat Builds: Swords & Fighting Styles">

While fruits provide flashy abilities, your primary damage output against high-level bosses often comes from a combination of Swords and Fighting Styles. The **haze seas trello** lists specific drop rates for legendary weapons, which are essential for players aiming for the max level of 2150.

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Progression Roadmap: From First Sea to Endgame">

Progressing in Haze Seas is structured around island-hopping. Each island features a set of quests and a boss that drops specific accessories or weapons. To reach the Second Sea, players must complete the main questline in the First Sea and reach the level threshold required to challenge the high-level bosses at Marine HQ and Thriller Boat.

  <Step num="1" title="Starter Phase (Level 1-300)">

  <Step num="2" title="Mid-Game Grind (Level 300-900)">

  <Step num="4" title="Endgame First Sea (Level 1500-2150)">

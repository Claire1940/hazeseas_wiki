<Section icon={Map} title="Haze Seas Sea Beast Location and Overview">

The **haze seas sea beast** (frequently referred to as the Red Sea Beast) represents one of the most formidable mid-to-late game challenges in the game. Classified as a Level 700 Superboss, this massive aquatic reptile demands a solid understanding of positioning and defensive timing. While its raw damage output can easily overwhelm unprepared players, its highly telegraphed combat mechanics make it an excellent target for consistent farming.

Finding the boss requires navigating to the outer reaches of the map. It spawns in the Rocky Pillars region, situated in close proximity to the Crew Flag. Because this area features vertical terrain and open water, managing your stamina and platform positioning is crucial before initiating the encounter.

<Section icon={Swords} title="Attack Patterns and Combat Strategies">

Surviving the **haze seas sea beast** encounter requires memorizing its strict three-move attack cycle. Unlike many other bosses that select moves randomly, this superboss executes its skills in a fixed, consecutive loop. Once you understand this sequence, you can predict exactly when to dodge, when to enable your defensive abilities, and when to unleash your highest-damage skills.

The boss utilizes three distinct moves: the Aquatic Fireball, the Roar, and the Sea Beam. Because they always trigger in this exact order, the rhythm of the fight remains highly consistent.

| Attack Name | Damage Value | Hitbox Size | Move Properties & Mechanical Effects |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Aquatic Fireball** | 2500 Damage | Very Large | Giant blue projectile with a long, telegraphed windup |
| **Roar** | 0 Damage | Medium | AoE stun that completely disables Observation Haki |
| **Sea Beam** | 1500 Damage | Small | Rapid, long-range laser with a very short startup time |

<Callout type="warning" title="Haki Disruption">
The Roar attack deals zero direct damage but is highly dangerous because it disables your Observation Haki. If you do not react quickly, the subsequent Sea Beam will likely hit you directly.
</Callout>

To successfully navigate this cycle without taking damage, follow this tactical dodging sequence during the fight:

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Dodge the Aquatic Fireball">
    As the boss begins its telegraphed windup, prepare to dash laterally. Due to the massive hitbox of the blue fireball, standard movement is rarely enough. Use a well-timed dodge or teleport to clear the blast radius.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Absorb or Avoid the Roar">
    The Roar cannot be easily avoided if you are in close proximity. Accept the brief stun, but immediately prepare for your next defensive input. Keep your fingers ready to reactivate your utility skills.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Re-enable Observation Haki">
    The instant the Roar stun ends, manually reactivate your Observation Haki. Doing this immediately ensures that the incoming fast-moving Sea Beam will be automatically dodged by your character.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Unleash Ranged Counterattacks">
    With the Sea Beam avoided and the boss entering its brief recovery window, dump your highest-damage ranged skills. Once the window closes, prepare to repeat Step 1 as the cycle restarts with the Aquatic Fireball.
  </Step>
</Steps>

This strategy revolves around the specific pillar where the Sky Walk Trainer NPC resides. Because of its height and distance from the water, it serves as an ideal safe zone.
